# Florida Real Estate Contract Statutory Requirements

This document outlines the statutory requirements derived from Florida law for real estate purchase contracts, listing agreements, and related addenda. All templates are based exclusively on these legal requirements.

## Sources

### Primary Statutes
- **Florida Statutes Chapter 475** - Real Estate Brokers and Salespersons
- **Florida Statutes Chapter 689** - Conveyances of Land and Declarations of Trust
- **Florida Statutes Chapter 720** - Homeowners' Associations
- **Florida Statutes § 404.056** - Radon Gas Notification
- **Florida Statutes § 553.996** - Energy Efficiency Rating Disclosure
- **24 CFR Part 35** - Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (Federal)

### Case Law
- **Johnson v. Davis, 480 So.2d 625 (Fla. 1985)** - Seller's duty to disclose material defects

## Purchase Contract Required Disclosures

### 1. Radon Gas Notification (§ 404.056(5)) - MANDATORY
**Effective:** Ongoing
**Requirement:** Must be provided at time of, or prior to, contract execution.

**Required Language:**
> "RADON GAS: Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that, when it has accumulated in a building in sufficient quantities, may present health risks to persons who are exposed to it over time. Levels of radon that exceed federal and state guidelines have been found in buildings in Florida. Additional information regarding radon and radon testing may be obtained from your county health department."

**Exceptions:**
- Unimproved properties (vacant land)
- Residential transient occupancy of 45 days or less

---

### 2. Property Tax Disclosure (§ 689.261) - MANDATORY
**Effective:** Ongoing
**Requirement:** Must be provided at or before execution of contract for sale.

**Required Language:**
> "BUYER SHOULD NOT RELY ON THE SELLER'S CURRENT PROPERTY TAXES AS THE AMOUNT OF PROPERTY TAXES THAT THE BUYER MAY BE OBLIGATED TO PAY IN THE YEAR SUBSEQUENT TO PURCHASE. A CHANGE OF OWNERSHIP OR PROPERTY IMPROVEMENTS TRIGGERS REASSESSMENTS OF THE PROPERTY THAT COULD RESULT IN HIGHER PROPERTY TAXES. IF YOU HAVE ANY QUESTIONS CONCERNING VALUATION, CONTACT THE COUNTY PROPERTY APPRAISER'S OFFICE FOR INFORMATION."

---

### 3. Flood Disclosure (§ 689.302) - MANDATORY
**Effective:** Original requirements since 2024; EXPANDED requirements effective October 1, 2025

**Original Requirements (pre-Oct 1, 2025):**
- Prior flood insurance claims filed for the property
- Federal assistance received due to flood damage

**Expanded Requirements (Oct 1, 2025+):** [RECENT CHANGE]
- Knowledge of any flooding that damaged the property during ownership
- Any flood insurance claims filed for the property
- Any assistance received (federal, state, local, or private) for flood damage

**Definition of "Flooding":**
A general or temporary condition of partial or complete inundation of the property caused by:
- Unusual and rapid accumulation of runoff or surface waters from any established water source (river, stream, drainage ditch)
- Sustained periods of standing water resulting from rainfall

**Timing:** Must be provided at or before contract execution.

---

### 4. Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (24 CFR Part 35) - CONDITIONAL
**Effective:** Ongoing (Federal requirement)
**Applies to:** Properties built before 1978

**Requirements:**
- Disclose known lead-based paint and/or lead-based paint hazards
- Provide EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family From Lead in Your Home"
- Provide 10-day opportunity to conduct risk assessment (may be waived)
- Disclose any available reports or records

---

### 5. HOA/Community Disclosure (§ 720.401) - CONDITIONAL
**Effective:** Ongoing
**Applies to:** Properties within a homeowners' association

**Required Disclosure Content:**
1. Purchaser will be obligated to be a member of a homeowners' association
2. Restrictive covenants govern use and occupancy
3. Purchaser will be obligated to pay assessments (may change periodically)
4. Purchaser should refer to covenants and governing documents before purchasing

**Cancellation Rights:**
- If disclosure not provided before contract execution, buyer may cancel within 3 days of receipt or prior to closing
- Waiver of this right is void
- Right terminates at closing

**Website Requirements (Jan 1, 2025):** [RECENT CHANGE]
Associations with 100+ parcels must post articles of incorporation and bylaws on website or mobile app.

---

### 6. Energy Efficiency Rating Disclosure (§ 553.996) - MANDATORY
**Effective:** Ongoing
**Requirement:** Must notify purchaser of option to obtain energy-efficiency rating at or before contract execution.

**Required Information:**
- How to analyze the building's energy-efficiency rating
- Comparisons to statewide averages for new and existing construction
- Information concerning methods to improve energy-efficiency
- Notice that rating may qualify purchaser for energy-efficient mortgage

---

### 7. Johnson v. Davis Material Defect Disclosure - MANDATORY (Common Law)
**Effective:** Since 1985
**Requirement:** Seller must disclose known facts materially affecting property value that are not readily observable and unknown to buyer.

**Elements:**
1. Seller has knowledge of a defect
2. Defect materially affects property value
3. Defect is not readily observable
4. Defect is unknown to buyer
5. Seller failed to disclose

**Note:** "As is" clauses do NOT waive this duty.

---

### 8. Disclosures NOT Required (§ 689.25)
The following are NOT material facts requiring disclosure:
- HIV/AIDS status of occupant
- Property was site of homicide, suicide, or death

## Earnest Money/Escrow Requirements (Chapter 475)

### Deposit Timeline
- Broker must deposit earnest money within **3 business days** of receipt

### Escrow Account Requirements
- Must be held in insured escrow/trust account at:
  - Bank
  - Savings and loan association
  - Trust company
  - Credit union
  - Title company with trust powers
- At least one broker must be signatory on account

### Record Keeping
- Maintain records for at least 5 years
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Discrepancies must be reported to FREC within 30 days

## Listing Agreement Requirements (Chapter 475)

### Brokerage Relationships (§ 475.278)
**Allowed:**
- Transaction broker
- Single agent

**Prohibited:**
- Disclosed dual agent
- Nondisclosed dual agent

### Single Agent Disclosure Requirements
- Must be in writing
- Must be provided before or at time of entering listing agreement
- If incorporated in other documents, must be same size type or larger and conspicuously placed
- First sentence must be in uppercase bold type

### Fiduciary Duties (Single Agent)
1. Loyalty
2. Confidentiality
3. Obedience
4. Full disclosure
5. Accounting
6. Skill, care, and diligence

### Contract Requirements (§ 475.25)
- **Definite expiration date required**
- **No automatic renewal provisions**
- No provision requiring seller to notify broker of intent to cancel after expiration

## Escalator Addendum Requirements

**Note:** No specific statutory requirements, but best practices from case law and industry standards:

### Required Elements
1. **Maximum Purchase Price Cap** - Must specify ceiling price buyer is willing to pay
2. **Escalation Increment** - Amount buyer will exceed competing offers by
3. **Bona Fide Offer Requirement** - Must reference legitimate competing offers only
4. **Proof of Competing Offer** - Seller should provide copy of competing offer triggering escalation

### Best Practices
- Require proof of funds for escalation amount
- Specify what constitutes a "bona fide" competing offer
- Define timeline for seller to provide proof of competing offer
- Address appraisal contingency interaction

## Recent Changes Summary (For UI Highlighting)

### October 1, 2025 Changes
1. **Flood Disclosure Expansion (§ 689.302)**
   - Now requires disclosure of "knowledge of flooding" (not just insurance claims)
   - Expands assistance disclosure to include state, local, and private sources
   - New landlord disclosure requirements for leases 1 year+

2. **Commercial Lease Sales Tax Elimination**
   - No longer required to collect/pay sales tax on commercial rent

### January 1, 2025 Changes
1. **HOA Website Requirements (§ 720.401)**
   - Associations with 100+ parcels must post governing documents online
